INPUT (sequence of tokens)
   |
   v
[Embedding Layer]  (high precision, NOT quantized)
   |
   v
FOR each of L Transformer layers (L = 12 to 96 depending on model size, see Table 5):
   |
   +--> [LayerNorm]
   +--> [Multi-Head Attention]
   |       +--> BitLinear (Q projection, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |       +--> BitLinear (K projection, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |       +--> BitLinear (V projection, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |       +--> [Attention computation]  (softmax(QK^T/sqrt(d)) @ V, high precision)
   |       +--> BitLinear (Output projection, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |
   +--> [Residual connection]  (high precision)
   |
   +--> [LayerNorm]
   +--> [Feed-Forward Network]
   |       +--> BitLinear (first linear, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |       +--> [GELU activation]
   |       +--> BitLinear (second linear, 1-bit weights, 8-bit activations)
   |
   +--> [Residual connection]  (high precision)
   |
   v
[Final LayerNorm]
   |
   v
[Output Linear]  (high precision, for sampling probabilities)
   |
   v
OUTPUT (logits for next token)

Notes:
- BitLinear = LayerNorm -> Absmax quantize activations -> matmul with +-1 weights -> rescale (beta*gamma/Qb).
- Only the linear projections inside attention and FFN are binarized; embeddings, output
  projection, residuals, and normalization stay high precision (see paper Section 2, "why
  BitNet leaves other components high-precision").
